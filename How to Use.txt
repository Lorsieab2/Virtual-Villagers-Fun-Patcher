VIRTUAL VILLAGERS FUN PATCHER

PHYSICAL POPULATION LIMITS

A New Home and The Lost Children have 256 stock villager records.
The Secret City, The Tree of Life, and New Believers have 150 stock records.
Experimental Expanded 256 Villagers structurally adds room for another 106
records and expands saved-villager tables in VV3-VV5.

1. Double-click "Launch Virtual Villagers Fun Patcher.bat".
2. Choose one of the four patch styles:
   - Collection Progression Max Pop:
     Collections still raise the cap and are needed to reach the absolute
     maximum. The Secret City also retains its level-3 magic bonus.
   - Immediate Fixed Max Pop:
     The absolute maximum is available immediately. Collections no longer
     affect it, and The Secret City's magic tech no longer affects it.
   - Experimental Expanded 256 Villagers:
     VV3-VV5 expand from 150 to 256 physical records; VV1-VV2 use their
     existing 256. The cap is immediate and collections do not change it.
     The differently named modified EXEs use separate executable-named save
     folders. Long-play to a full 256 remains experimental.
   - Experimental Expanded 256 - Collection Progression:
     Uses the same expanded 256-record layout, but collections keep their
     original population bonuses and are required to reach 256. The Secret
     City's level-3 Magic bonus also remains active.
3. Optional for A New Home: check "School Lessons Grant Skill",
   "Continue Research at Max Technologies", and/or
   "Reenable F6 Clothing Change Cheat".
4. Optional for The Lost Children: check "Easier Healing Mastery" and/or
   "Teaching Children Grants Skill".
5. Optional for The Secret City: check
   "Nature Level 1 Improves Honey Refill".
6. Optional for The Tree of Life: check
   "Complete Fish Scales = Golden Fish in Nets".
7. Optional for New Believers: check "Heathen Mommy Puzzle Restoration" and/or
   "Easier Devotee Training" and/or
   "Statue Drops: Polishing or Honoring" and/or
   "VV4 Nursery School Divisor Parity".
7. Open "One Game" or "All 5 Games".

PATCH ALL FIVE AT ONCE

1. Use the five separate fields to choose one folder per game. Each folder must
   contain that game's ORIGINAL EXE.
2. Or use "Find All 5 in Parent Folder..." when the EXEs are in the selected
   folder or one folder below it.
3. Click "Validate All 5", "Dry Run All 5", or "Patch All 5".
4. Use the clickable "Vanilla folder" or "Modified folder" link on any row
   to open the original folder or its separate copied-and-modified folder.
5. After patching, the confirmation window provides clear clickable Vanilla
   Folder and Modded Folder links for all five games.

PATCH ONE GAME

1. Browse to one ORIGINAL game EXE.
2. Click Validate, Dry Run, or Create Modified EXE.
3. Use "Open Vanilla EXE Folder" or "Open Modified EXE Folder" to open the
   selected game's folder in File Explorer.

OPTIONAL PATCH SELECTION

Use "Select All Patches" to turn on every optional fun patch at once. Use
"Deselect All Patches" to turn them all off. These buttons do not change the
selected population patch style.

For each game, the patcher creates one complete sibling copy named
"(Game name) - Modded". The copied folder keeps every original file and
subfolder, including the stock EXE, and adds the separately named modified EXE
and verification log. The copy is verified before it is committed. Applying
another style refreshes the same short Modded folder after confirmation.
Original folders and EXEs are never changed, renamed, replaced, or deleted. The
patcher remembers the selected style, single-game EXE path, and all five folder
paths. Vanilla and Modded links open their respective folders.

SAFE MULTIPLE BIRTHS

All five games normally check the cap before deciding singleton/twins/triplets.
All four styles prevent overflow at the selected mode's slot ceiling:
- 3+ spaces: multiples work normally.
- 2 spaces: a rolled triplet becomes twins.
- 1 space: a rolled twin or triplet becomes a singleton.
- 0 spaces: the normal cap check blocks the birth.

Population-adding Island Events are protected in all five games too.
Repeated arrivals stop at the physical ceiling. VV4 and VV5 Abandoned
Infants is reduced below six when fewer than six physical slots remain.
Events that remove villagers are unchanged.

Only the five exact researched stock builds are supported. Unknown, modified,
corrupt, duplicate, or incorrectly assigned EXEs are refused.

VV2 EASIER HEALING MASTERY

When a healer or a villager who prefers Healing has no sick villager to treat,
the patch sends that villager into the game's existing plant-study work state.
That persistent state is used during normal play and time catch-up. Normal
healing, sickness, food, skill gains, and plant availability are unchanged.

VV1 REENABLE F6 CLOTHING CHANGE CHEAT

Press F6 to advance the selected active villager to the next stock outfit. The
cycle uses clothing indices 0 through 19 and wraps from 19 back to 0. Nothing
changes if no active villager is selected. The patch does not change heads,
sex, age, skills, health, jobs, actions, or clothing assets. All non-F6 keys
retain their original behavior.

VV2 TEACHING CHILDREN GRANTS SKILL

Whenever the stock Teaching Children activity enrolls a child as Attending
Lessons, that child gains one point in one equally random skill: Farming,
Building, Research, Healing, or Parenting. Skills remain capped at 100. The
patch does not change who teaches, who attends, or how often lessons happen.

VV3 NATURE LEVEL 1 IMPROVES HONEY REFILL

Nature level 1 or higher improves honey regeneration by the same proportional
amount used by the game's fruit-tree refill routine. Nature level 0 keeps the
stock honey rate. The one-hour honey update threshold and 3,000-unit cap remain
unchanged. The patch does not change honey harvesting, initial honey, the honey
display, fruit trees, or Nature technology progression.

VV5 HEATHEN MOMMY PUZZLE RESTORATION

The naturally enabled reference creates a 29th, tag-17 Heathen mother and gives
her one nursing baby. The modern new-game initializer omits both, although it
retains the puzzle class, trigger, completion logic, actions, text, and locked
and solved images. This option restores the exact mother-and-baby sequence for
newly created villages and restores the separate 17th-puzzle drawing branch.
The pair uses two physical population slots. Existing saves are not
retroactively given a new mother.

VV5 EASIER DEVOTEE TRAINING

Any villager with positive Devotion skill can spontaneously choose the game's
original Honoring action. Normally, the autonomous opportunity is confined to
the Retired Chief job state, while other devotees have to be dropped on the
upgradeable statue to begin Honoring. The patch checks actual Devotion skill
instead and keeps the stock idle timing, action queue, and skill-gain behavior.
It does not directly grant skill or change conversion, statue upgrades, manual
assignment, or skill thresholds. Spreading the Word remains a Retired Chief
activity and is not assigned to ordinary devotees.

VV5 STATUE DROPS: POLISHING OR HONORING

Dropping a villager on the upgradeable or completed statue chooses with equal
odds between the original Polishing the Statue and Honoring activities. The
patch changes only the manual drop selection and keeps both stock action queues.
It does not change autonomous work, skill gain amounts, statue upgrades, or
Retired Chief activities.
